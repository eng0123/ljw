# Graduation Poster Prompt Example

## Scenario

Create a cinematic graduation poster based on a real portrait reference.

## Input fields

- Portrait reference: provided by user
- Theme: graduation, growth, future, memory
- Style: cinematic poster, soft watercolor, double exposure
- Output: vertical poster for social media and print

## Prompt template

Use the provided portrait as the visual base. Keep the original face identity and natural facial structure. Do not invent a new face. Create a vertical cinematic graduation poster with a large side-profile or half-profile silhouette. Inside the silhouette, build a double-exposure narrative world connected to campus life, reading, writing, future exploration, and personal growth.

Include symbolic elements such as books, handwritten notes, campus architecture, soft flowers, moonlight, butterflies, and a distant future city. Keep the composition minimal, poetic, clean, and balanced. Use soft watercolor texture, paper grain, gentle haze, warm vintage lighting, and restrained typography.

If text is needed, use minimal English title text only, such as:

To the Unwritten
Where words begin, the world unfolds.

## Constraints

- Preserve the subject's facial structure and expression.
- Avoid generic AI beauty-face transformation.
- Avoid cluttered collage composition.
- Avoid too many duplicated flowers or decorative props.
- Do not include other clear people.
- Use a soft, clean, collectible poster style.

## Review checklist

- Does the subject still look like the original person?
- Is the silhouette clearly related to the person?
- Is the graduation theme visible within three seconds?
- Is the image poetic rather than messy?
